        Scope of Work (from TPF #1633)

        Summary; see the solicitation for the full text.

        • Gap analysis of each state’s corridor data feeds; implement solutions to meet a minimum multi‑state service standard.
        • Standardize and normalize data formats (XML/JSON) across participating states; align with next‑gen Traffic Management Data Dictionary (TDMD).
        • Draft consistent, shared data policies and governance that work across agencies and coalitions.
        • Engage mapping providers, vehicle OEMs, and ELD/fleet platforms to align schemas for TIM and traveler information messages.
        • Pilot and evaluate open data exchanges (e.g., truck parking availability) and expand beyond initial SMART grant pilots.
        • Deploy selected, scalable solutions on I‑80 and I‑35; evaluate device interoperability and adoption approaches.
        • Provide training, validation tools, and tech‑transfer artifacts to speed implementation and reduce costs.
        • Develop a formal charter using MOUs/LOUs for long‑term corridor governance and coordinated grant activities.
